About Me

For quite a while, I have been extremely interested in computers and circuitry. I have been writing software for around 7 years, and have been tinkering with electronics for approximately the same amount of time.

My interest in amateur radio came in 2018, when I discovered with world of software-defined radios. In the time between discovering amateur radio and getting licensed I:

  • Experimented with decoding images from NOAA-15, NOAA-18, and NOAA-19
  • Listened in to a few nets held on local repeaters using my SDRs
  • Begun to learn morse code
  • Continued to learn about various forms of radio communication through DEFCON talks, and presentations from various radio clubs online

After getting licensed and receiving my callsign, I immediately got on the air (quite literally) and started participating in local nets, occasional simplex QSOs, and more.

I now primarily operate on 40 and 20 meters, using CW and RTTY. I have come to enjoy morse code, and I actively try to meet new people on the air each day (sticking to CW for now). After a few months of CW contacts, my morse code skills have become what I call "functional". I still struggle a bit when sending words I don't use often, but I stay persistent to hopefully become morse-code-fluent.

The "Shack"

My ham shack is currently my bedroom, where I have the following radios:

  • Elecraft KX2
  • Baofeng UV-5R
  • CountyComm GP-5
  • Nooelec NESDR Nano 3
  • RTL-SDR RTL2832U
  • KC9ON Pixie and Pixie Switch kit

My antenna is a 40m quarter-wave dipole, or a 20m EFHW (depending on how I set it) that is made of stripped ethernet cables I pulled from my school's tech dumpsters. The wires are wrapped around my bedroom in opposite directions, and work well enough. Once summer comes, I hope to set up a much larger EFHW antenna outdoors that should reduce my current antenna losses, and allow me to use some more bands.
